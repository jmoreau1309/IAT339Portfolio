Project Goal

This feature integration project is from my interaction design course. The idea was to select an application, perform user research, and then come up with design concepts for an application feature to create a mockup for. I selected Discord, a communication application generally used by gamers, school groups, and friend groups alike. Initially to better understand these different user groups I interviewed a handful of people from each who use discord and found out why they use it and if they have pain points or any wishes for the app. From there I began to formulate some initial design concepts and proceeded to create lo-fidelity wireframes.

Feature Ideation

My two features, pictured below, are also accompanied by a portion of the wireframes I designed for them. The first idea was to address the fact that some users do not have or can not have their cameras on while in a video call. The specific target audience for this feature is PC gamers who either do not have a webcam on their PC or having it on makes their computer and game lag. This feature allows the user to use their phone camera as their camera while remaining in the discord call on their PC in case their are streaming or wish to remain on their PC.

This final project for my graphic design class was done in groups of four and gave the teams a designer precedent and client to use to create a microsite. Using our Designer Precedent, Wolfgang Weingart, we created advertising posters for our client, the Holland Festival, using Weingart's design principles. The poster designs would then be used to inspire our microsite, which would primarily be a space for users to view shows and purchase tickets.
